Opportunity areas for a new AI fitness app
Highest signal — 9/9 apps
Rock-solid reliability as a core feature
Every single app generates its worst reviews from bugs, crashes, and data loss — often after updates. Multiple reviewers across multiple apps explicitly say they would give 5 stars were it not for reliability failures. Being the app that never loses a workout is a genuine differentiator, not a baseline.
Highest signal — 9/9 apps
Transparent, honest pricing model
Every app frustrates users with paywalls hit mid-onboarding, billing surprises, auto-renewals with no reminders, and refund stonewalling. A genuinely fair model — a useful free tier, clear pricing shown upfront, easy cancellation — would be an immediate differentiator in a category where trust is chronically low.
Very high signal — 8/9 apps
Genuine AI coaching, not just logging
Eight of nine apps are criticised as "just a tracker." The "AI" label raises an expectation of real decisions — adaptive loads, intelligent progression, sessions that respond to how you're actually doing. No app in this dataset convincingly delivers that. The coaching gap is the biggest product opportunity in the category.
Very high signal — 7/9 apps
Frictionless subscription management
Surprise auto-renewals, email-only cancellation flows, and blanket no-refund policies generate some of the most emotionally charged 1-star reviews across the dataset — including Guardian consumer complaint coverage and Trustpilot escalations. Clean, standard, app-store-native subscription management would neutralise a significant source of brand damage.
Strong signal — 6/9 apps
Real-world context awareness
Six apps prescribe equipment users don't own, exercises incompatible with busy gyms, or circuits that require multiple stations simultaneously. An AI that genuinely understands your actual setup — what you have, where you are, what's available right now — and adjusts accordingly solves a persistent daily frustration.
Strong signal — 6/9 apps
A plan that grows with the user
Five apps share an identical churn arc: excellent for beginners, abandoned by intermediates who feel the programming no longer challenges them. An AI with genuinely distinct modes for different experience levels — and smooth, earned transitions between them — retains users through the phase where every existing app loses them.
Strong signal — 4/9 apps
Preferences and constraints that actually stick
Fitbod, Shred, Gravl, and JuggernautAI all share the same failure: disliked exercises return despite explicit downvotes, and injury constraints are only partially respected. An AI that truly learns and enforces what you cannot or will not do — and never forgets it — builds a level of trust that no current app provides.
Emerging signal — 3/9 apps
Recovery and safety awareness
Freeletics, JuggernautAI, and Fitbod all generate complaints about excessive volume, reckless intensity ramp-up, and no recovery guidance. As AI coaching gets more serious, an app that actively monitors overtraining signals and modulates load intelligently — rather than just pushing harder — occupies a meaningful and underserved position.
